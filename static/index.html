How to Play

Tactical hex-based space combat. Plot burns, ride gravity, and outfly the enemy.

Phases Movement Gravity Combat Ships Ordnance Victory Legend Controls

Turn Phases

1Astrogation — plot fuel burns for your ships
2Ordnance — launch mines, torpedoes, or nukes (one item per ship)
3Movement — ships move simultaneously; gravity from last turn applies now
4Combat — gun attacks, counterattacks, and planetary defense resolve
5Transfers — (optional) exchange fuel or cargo between ships at the same hex
Both players complete their full turn sequence before a new game turn begins.

Movement & Fuel

Ships maintain velocity — they drift in the same direction every turn unless you burn.
A burn costs 1 fuel and shifts velocity by 1 hex in any of 6 directions. Click/tap the arrow around your ship to set it.
Warships can overload: spend 2 fuel total for a 2-hex velocity change in one turn (once per stopover).
Out of fuel? You still drift — you just can't change course.
Ships that drift off the map are eliminated.
[Screenshot: A ship with its cyan velocity arrow pointing ahead, and burn arrows around it showing the 6 possible acceleration directions]

Gravity & Landing

Gravity hexes show an arrow toward their planet. A ship entering a gravity hex has its velocity deflected by 1 hex — but the deflection applies on the next turn.
Weak gravity (moons — Luna, Io, Callisto, Ganymede): you may choose to ignore a single weak-gravity hex. Two consecutive weak-gravity hexes of the same body make the second deflection mandatory.
Orbit is not a special state — it emerges naturally from a speed-1 course looping around the gravity ring.
To land: achieve orbit, then spend 1 fuel to touch down. Landing at a friendly base repairs all damage, restores fuel, and resupplies ordnance.
Any other contact with a planet or moon is a crash (eliminated).
[Screenshot: A gravity well around a planet with an arrow showing how a ship's course bends into orbit on the next turn]

Combat

Compare combined attacker combat factors vs. defender to get odds. Roll 1d6 and apply modifiers.
Range penalty−1 per hex of distance (closest approach to target)
Velocity penalty−1 per hex of relative speed above 2
The defender counterattacks first — before taking damage. Ships at the same hex sharing the same course may join the counterattack.

Damage Rules

Disabled ships drift and cannot maneuver, attack, or launch ordnance.
Damage is cumulative — 6 or more total disabled turns = eliminated.
Each ship repairs 1 disabled turn at the end of each of its own turns.
Dreadnaughts may still fire even when fully disabled. Orbital Bases may fire at D1 damage only.

Ordnance

Mines mass 10

Inherit the launching ship's velocity. The launching ship must immediately change course to leave the mine hex. Active for 5 turns. Cannot be shot down. Detonate when any ship or ordnance passes through.

Torpedoes mass 20

Warships only. Inherit launch ship's velocity then may accelerate 1–2 hexes on the launch turn. Hit one target then continue moving if they miss. Active for 5 turns.

Nukes mass 20

Inherit launch ship's velocity. Destroy everything in the detonated hex automatically. Guns and planetary defense may intercept at 2:1 odds — any disabling result destroys the nuke. Active for 5 turns.
[Screenshot: A mine and torpedo on the map with their dashed trails, showing an enemy ship approaching the mine field]

Win Conditions

Fleet wiped outEliminate all enemy ships — used in most combat scenarios
Land on targetReach and land on the designated planet (Bi-Planetary, Blockade Runner)
EscortLand on the target planet with passengers aboard (Convoy, Evacuation)
EscapePilgrim transports flee past Jupiter — Enforcers win by capturing or destroying the fugitive ship (Escape)
Grand TourVisit Sol, Mercury, Venus, Terra, Mars, Jupiter, Io, and Callisto then return home first (no combat)

Map Symbols

  • Hex grid — the playfield.
  • Gravity — ships drift one step this way each turn. Brighter = stronger.
  • Body — sun, planet, or moon.
  • Ships — cyan = yours, orange = enemy. Octagon = orbital station.
  • Velocity — where a ship coasts next without burning.
  • Trail — recent movement path; fades over time.
  • Ordnance trail — torpedo or nuke path history.
  • Station — cyan = yours, orange = enemy, green = neutral.
  • Sensor ring — detection range around your ship or bases.
  • Threat zone — hexes near enemy bases; dangerous to approach.
